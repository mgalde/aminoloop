The Process

Closing the Nutrient Loop

Aquamation — also known as alkaline hydrolysis — is a water-based cremation process that uses warm water and an alkali solution to gently return a body to its natural elements. Unlike flame cremation, it preserves the rich organic compounds that once formed the foundation of life. Those compounds are the foundation of Amino Loop.

01

Aquamation

In partnership with Aqua Crossings in Tucson, Arizona, pets are honored through aquamation — a gentle process using a warm solution of water and potassium hydroxide. Soft tissue dissolves over several hours while bone structure is preserved and returned to the family. The result is a sterile, nutrient-dense liquid byproduct that would otherwise be treated as wastewater.

02

Collection & Analysis

The effluent — a clear to pale amber liquid — is rich in free amino acids, peptides, phosphorus, nitrogen, and trace minerals accumulated over the animal's lifetime. Each batch is carefully collected, laboratory-tested for composition and purity, and documented to ensure consistent quality and regulatory compliance before any further processing.

03

Processing & Refinement

Through proprietary filtration and concentration methods, the effluent is refined into a stable, shelf-stable liquid fertilizer. pH is carefully balanced and nutrient ratios are standardized to meet agricultural specifications. The final product is a premium, naturally-derived biostimulant — ready for direct soil application or foliar use.

04

Distribution & Application

Amino Loop's liquid fertilizer is available by contract to agricultural operations, nurseries, urban farms, and sustainable landscaping projects across Arizona and the greater Southwest. Contract holders receive consistent, scheduled deliveries of product — a reliable, ethical alternative to synthetic fertilizers derived from petroleum or industrial phosphate mining.

The Opportunity

Why Amino Loop?

Circular by Design

Amino Loop's model is inherently circular. What is currently treated as wastewater becomes a valuable agricultural input — embodying the principles of a zero-waste economy and regenerative agriculture. The more aquamations occur, the more input Amino Loop has to work with.

Consistent Supply Stream

With the companion animal market generating hundreds of thousands of aquamations annually — and the industry growing — the feedstock for Amino Loop's product is consistent, scalable, and growing independently of seasonal or agricultural variables.

Proprietary Market Position

Amino Loop sits at a unique intersection of the pet aftercare industry and sustainable agriculture — a combination with minimal existing competition and significant regulatory tailwinds as synthetic fertilizer scrutiny and ESG investment criteria continue to grow.

Early-Stage Opportunity

As an early-stage venture, Amino Loop offers investors the opportunity to enter at ground level. Current rounds are focused on building processing infrastructure, expanding distribution, and securing agricultural partnerships across Arizona and the Southwest.

About

A Partnership
Rooted in Purpose

Amino Loop was founded on a simple premise: that nothing is truly waste when viewed through the right lens. Born from a partnership with Aqua Crossings — Tucson's premier aquamation provider — Amino Loop captures the nutrient-rich effluent from the water cremation process and transforms it into an agricultural asset.

Our founding team brings together expertise in sustainable agriculture, biochemistry, and small-batch production. We believe the future of fertilizer is not mined from rock phosphate or synthesized from fossil fuels — it is regenerated from the organic matter already cycling through our world.

Based in Tucson, Arizona, we are proud to be a desert-born company focused on the sustainable stewardship of land and legacy. The Southwest's agricultural community deserves inputs that reflect the values of the land itself — resilient, efficient, and naturally derived.
